How to Know if you are making progress in programming

Measuring programming skills is hard task to do, this is because it does not have a physical way of figuring out. Sometimes we engage in projects and at some point, when we look at half-finished projects, it look more like a failure than a progress. Here are few tips you can gauge yourself with:
  1. The best way to measure your progress in programming is by reviewing the old assignments, algorithms and some problems that you had coded in the past, and see if you could have a better solution to counter those problems.
  2. Progress is also depicted by the speed and the duration for instance; solving hard problems faster than before
  3. When you look at your previous codes and it seems like shit to you, thus making you to have questions like; "What the hell was i thinking?"
  4. When you came to a point of learning libraries, technologies and APIs and additional of language specific features, you will come to realization that you had done it the wrong or a long way, for instance maybe you had written more than 500 lines of codes that can only be replaced by 20 lines of codes when using a library.
  5. When you look at your previous projects, you will discover most parts of your code is junkie and requires more edition to come out clean
  6. Many instructors recommend using physical progress since it feels more rewarding than any other, one of the best way to do this is by using todo lists. For a programmer who engages with large or small projects Trello is the best site to measure up your skills and your achievements

7. Engage yourself in projects that fit in to the level and the stage you are. This will look like you are measuring and putting the skills that you have learnt in to practice for instance:

(Beginner =>Intermediate => Pro) Levels

Whe you feel comfortable with beginner-friendly projects, take another step to the intermediate level.
